Day 01

English Masterclass: Pronouns, To Be, & Basic Questions

1) Subject Pronouns

Pronouns are used to indicate who is involved in a conversation.

Singular Plural
I am John. We are Josh and Gabriela.
You are a student. You are students.
He is Josh. They are boys and girls.
She is Emma. They are dogs, aren't they?
It is a dog / It is a radio.

2) Verb "To Be" (Am/Is/Are)

The verb "to be" is usually used to express permanent characteristics or temporary statuses of people, animals, or objects.

A) Affirmative Form

Singular (Full & Contraction) Plural (Full & Contraction)
I am Emma = I'm We are happy = We're
You are from Florida = You're You are students = You're
He is an engineer = He's They are students = They're
She is a teacher = She's
It is a company = It's

B) Negative Form

Add NOT after the verb.
  • I am not Emma! (I'm not)
  • You are not from Florida! (You aren't)
  • He is not a kung fu teacher! (He isn't)
  • She isn't a very beautiful teacher. (She isn't)
  • It is not a problem! (It's not / It isn't)
  • We are not friends. (We aren't)
  • They are not happy. (They aren't)

3) Questions (Interrogative)

The interrogative form is the opposite construction of the affirmative. The verb comes before the subject.

Stuart: Madam, is this the bathroom?

Attendant: No, this isn't. This is the cockpit.

John: Where are you from, Megan?

Megan: I am...

John: You are from Sydney, I remember.

D) YES/NO Questions & Short Answers

Are you...? Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
Is he...? Yes, he is. / No, he isn't.
Is she...? Yes, she is. / No, she isn't.
Are they...? Yes, they are. / No, they're not.

4) Articles: A / An / The

A is used almost every time (before consonants).

AN is used when the next word starts with a vowel sound (A, E, I, O, U).

THE is specific. Used in Singular and Plural.

  • Bill Gates is a billionaire and an American citizen.
  • Bill Gates is the founder of Microsoft.
  • Michael Schumacher and Ayrton Senna are formula 1 drivers.

5) Who / What / Where

  • Who - questions about people.
  • Where - questions about places, locations.
  • What - questions about objects, ideas, and actions.
