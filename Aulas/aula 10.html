1) Comparatives

When you compare two things (objects, people, cities, etc.) or more, you use comparative adjectives.

Examples:
  • Abbey Road studio is bigger than a personal studio.
  • Classical music is more beautiful than rap music.

Pay attention:

  • Short adjectives = Adjective + ER + than
  • Long adjectives = More + adjective + than
OldOlder than
SmallSmaller than
HappyHappier than
IntelligentMore intelligent than
ExtrovertedMore extroverted than
ExpensiveMore expensive than

Spelling Notes:

  • CVC (Consonant-Vowel-Consonant): Double the final consonant (big ‚Üí bigger, fat ‚Üí fatter).
  • Ending in Y: Change 'y' to 'i' (happy ‚Üí happier, funny ‚Üí funniest).
  • Irregular: good ‚Üí better, bad ‚Üí worse, far ‚Üí farther.

2) Superlatives

When you compare one thing with a group of the same type, you use a superlative adjective.

Examples:
  • Abbey Road is the largest studio in the world.
  • The violin has the most beautiful sound of all instruments.

Pay attention:

  • Short adjectives (1 or 2 syllables) = The + Adjective + EST
  • Long adjectives (2 or more syllables) = The most + Adjective
OldThe oldest
SmallThe smallest
HappyThe happiest
IntelligentThe most intelligent
ExtrovertedThe most extroverted
ExpensiveThe most expensive

3) Stative Verbs

Stative verbs are verbs that express a state rather than an action. They usually relate to thoughts, emotions, relationships, senses, states of being and measurements.

Examples: agree, be, belong to, believe, feel, hate, know, like, love, need, remember, see, smell, understand, want.
