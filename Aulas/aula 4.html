Simple Present Tense

üìò Simple Present

This verb form represents frequent actions, everyday activities and generalizations.

Examples:

  • I visit my grandmother on Sundays. (Verb - to visit)
  • Frank plays volleyball at school. (Verb - to play)
  • They go to the beach every summer. (Verb - to go)
  • She takes a shower every morning. (Verb - to take)
  • The sky is blue. (Verb - to be)

‚ö†Ô∏è Attention!

It is important to identify the subject of a sentence.

Examples:

  • Mary drives a Mercedes. > Subject = Mary (she)
  • The shop opens at 8 am. > Subject = The shop (it)
  • The bus leaves in the morning. > Subject = The bus (it)
  • Father and Mother play with the kids. > Subject = Father and Mother (they)

‚ö†Ô∏è Attention for He / She / It

  1. For verbs ending in sh, ch, ss, o or x, add es to the verb (e.g. teaches, washes, watches, kisses).
  2. For verbs ending in consonant + y, substitute y for i and add es to the verb (e.g. candies, tries, etc).
