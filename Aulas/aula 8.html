Forms

Verb 'to be' conjugated + going to + main verb (infinitive)
B) Negative The store isn't going to open at 9 am.
C) Interrogative Is the store going to open at 9 am?

Expressing time in the future: tomorrow, next week/year, soon, in a while, in a minute, after this class, tonight.

2) Quantifiers - Quantity Indicators

In English, to indicate quantity, we use the words below:

A) With Countable Nouns

(dogs, cars, people, houses, ideas, secrets)

  • Few -> I have few dollars. ($3)
  • A few -> I have a few dollars. ($10)
  • Many -> I have many dollars. ($100)
  • A lot -> I have a lot of dollars. ($1000)

B) With Uncountable Nouns

(water, paper, coffee)

  • Little -> I have little money. ($3)
  • A little -> I have a little money. ($10)
  • Much -> I have much money. ($100)
  • A lot -> I have a lot of money. ($1000)
Note:
â€¢ Countable nouns normally have a singular and a plural form (book - books).
â€¢ Uncountable nouns normally don't have a plural form (air, money, rice). Action words are uncountable (love, hate, sleep, eat).

3) Some Question Words

What / Where / When / Who / Why

Compare the structure and answers:

  • Yes/No: Do you dance? -> Yes, I do.
  • What: What type of dance do you do? -> Samba.
  • Where: Where do you dance? -> At a dance school and at parties.
  • When: When do you dance? -> Every weekend.
  • Who: Who do you dance with? -> My dance partner, Carlos.
  • Why: Why do you dance? -> Because I feel free.
