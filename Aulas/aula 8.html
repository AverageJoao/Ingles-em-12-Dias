English Masterclass: Simple Future & Quantifiers

1) Simple Future - "Be going to"

This verbal form expresses simple actions in the future (= will) and also plans in the future (‚â† will).

Examples:
  • They are going to call the police later. (= They will call the police later.)
  • I'm going to be a nurse when I grow up. (Plan)

2) Quantifiers - Quantity Indicators

In English, to indicate quantity, we use the words below:

A) With Countable Nouns
(dogs, cars, people, houses, ideas) 		B) With Uncountable Nouns
(water, paper, coffee)
Few
-> I have few dollars. ($3) 		Little
-> I have little money. ($3)
A few
-> I have a few dollars. ($10) 		A little
-> I have a little money. ($10)
Many
-> I have many dollars. ($100) 		Much
-> I have much money. ($100)
A lot
-> I have a lot of dollars. ($1000) 		A lot
-> I have a lot of money. ($1000)

Note:

  • Countable nouns normally have a singular and a plural form (book - books).
  • Uncountable nouns normally don't have a plural form (air, money, rice). Action words are uncountable (love, hate, sleep, eat).

3) Some Question Words

What Where When Who Why

Compare the structure and answers:

Yes/No: Do you dance? → Yes, I do.

What: What type of dance do you do? → Samba.

Where: Where do you dance? → At a dance school and at parties.

When: When do you dance? → Every weekend.

Who: Who do you dance with? → My dance partner, Carlos.

Why: Why do you dance? → Because I feel free.
