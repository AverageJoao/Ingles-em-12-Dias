English Class: There is / There are

Grammar: There is / There are

We use there is and there are to say that something exists.

üîµ There is: singular (one thing)

üîµ There are: plural (more than one thing)

A) Affirmative form

  • There is a round table in the kitchen.
  • There are two armchairs beside the dining table.
  • There is a frog in the bathroom!
  • There are many toys on the dining room floor.

Contracted forms:
There is = There's (e.g., There's a delicious chocolate cake).
*You cannot contract 'there are'.

B) Negative form

It is formed by adding not after is or are.

  • There is not any furniture in Mike's living room.
  • There are not sofas in Mike's living room.

Contracted forms:

There is not = There isn't

There are not = There aren't

C) Interrogative forms

To form a question we place is/are in front of there.

  • Is there any furniture in Mike's living room?
  • Are there sofas in his room?

D) Short answers

  • Is there a dog in your house? No, there isn't.
  • Are there sofas in Mike's living room? No, there aren't.
