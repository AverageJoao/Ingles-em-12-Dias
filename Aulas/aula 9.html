English Masterclass: Simple Past

Definition

The simple past expresses a completed action that happened at a specific time in the past.

Verbs List

Regular Verbs

Verbs that end in -ed.

to live lived
to start started
to visit visited
to watch watched
to play played

Irregular Verbs

Verbs that change form completely.

to be was, were
to write wrote
to go went
to do did
to have had
to speak spoke

Affirmative Sentences

Subject + Verb in Past + Complement

  • I studied English last week.
  • She watched TV yesterday.
  • They played video games last night.
  • He woke up at 6 am this morning.
  • They understood the grammar.
  • We went to TopWay yesterday in the afternoon.

Negative Sentences

Rule: Use did not (didn't) + Infinitive verb
  • I did not (didn't) eat pizza for lunch.
  • He didn't work last weekend.
  • You didn't like the movie last night.
  • The dog didn't catch the ball.
  • We didn't travel to Europe last year.
  • They didn't drink wine at the party.

Interrogative Sentences

Rule: Use Did + Subject + Infinitive verb...?
  • Did you eat breakfast this morning?
  • Did Brazil win the 2002 World Cup?
  • Did she go to school yesterday?
  • Did the dog eat the food?
  • Did he play football with Pel√©?
  • Did you do your homework?

Verb "to be" (was/were)

Interrogative Affirmative Negative
Were you happy? Yes, I was happy. No, I was not happy.
Was she an English student? Yes, she was an English student. No, she wasn't.
Were they British? Yes, they were British. No, they weren't.
