Day 07 - English Class

1) Simple Future - "Will" Future

The Simple Future expresses a spontaneous decision, an assumption with regard to the future, an action in the future that cannot be influenced or a prediction based on no evidence.

Examples:
  • "Oh, the trash is full. I'll take it out." (You didn't plan it; you just decided right now).
  • "You'll love Paris!" (This is your opinion, not a fact).
  • "I think the Green team will win the game." (Belief, not based on clear evidence).
  • "The sun will rise tomorrow at 6:15 AM."
  • "Next year, I will be 30."

A) Simple Future Forms

Affirmative (+) Negative (-) Interrogative (?)
SUBJECT + WILL + VERB SUBJECT + WILL NOT + VERB WILL + SUBJECT + VERB
Ex: I will travel to Rio soon. Ex: I will not travel to Rio soon. Ex: Will Jackie travel to Rio soon?
Contracted form: 'll Contracted form: won't Short Answers: Yes, she will. / No, she won't.

B) Uses of "Will" Future

  • A spontaneous decision: "Wait, I will help you with that!"
  • An opinion, hope, uncertainty: "She'll probably visit many beautiful places in Rio."
  • A promise: "I'll bring you a present from Rio."
  • A prediction (imagination/no evidence): "It won't rain during my holidays in Brazil."

2) Object Pronouns

Subject and object pronouns are used in everyday language. However, it can be a little tricky to remember which is which! Take a look:

Subject Pronouns (Takes Action) Object Pronouns (Receives Action)
IMe
YouYou
HeHim
SheHer
ItIt
WeUs
YouYou
TheyThem
The subject pronoun always takes action. The object pronoun is part of the activity, but it does not do any acting.

Examples in Context

Scenario: Faith is talking to Bella on the phone.

  • Faith (she) is the subject (she is talking), she is taking the action.
  • Bella (her) is the object of Faith's action; the talking is done to her, not by her.
  • Result: Faith is talking to Bella / She is talking to her.

More examples:

  • Faith will bring me a Brazilian souvenir.
  • I need to tell you something about Rio de Janeiro: it's wonderful!
  • It wasn't for her, it was for us!
