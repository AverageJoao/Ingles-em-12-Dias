Simple Present Tense

üìò Simple Present

This verb form represents frequent actions, everyday activities and generalizations.

Examples:

  • I visit my grandmother on Sundays. (Verb - to visit)
  • Frank plays volleyball at school. (Verb - to play)
  • They go to the beach every summer. (Verb - to go)
  • She takes a shower every morning. (Verb - to take)
  • The sky is blue. (Verb - to be)

‚ö†Ô∏è Rules for He / She / It

  1. For verbs ending in sh, ch, ss, o or x, add es (e.g. teaches, washes, watches, kisses).
  2. For verbs ending in consonant + y, substitute y for i and add es (e.g. candies, tries).
